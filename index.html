PacGirl

by: Aryahi Gupta

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I don't like hard shooting games, and am not very good at it, so I decided to make a less stressful game. PacMan is a classic arcade game, and I just wanted to put my own unique spin on it.

2. What I Learned

One suggestion that I received is to keep track of how many cherries the user has collected, as well as how many lives they use or have left. This made my game much better, and gave it more sense of purpose. Another issue that came up was making the instructions more specific, and how the player was confused by the different ghost colors and designs.

3. Architecture of My Game

My PacGirl runner class is the top of the hierarchy, which instantiates the GamePanel class, what deals with the high level game components. That then instantiates the ObjectManager class that contains the detailed game movements. And each of my objects, such as PacGirl and the ghosts, have their own class.

4. Technical Implementation

One aspect of my game that I'm proud of is the randomized ghost movement. The game starts out with six ghosts, and with multiple if and else statements, will navigate throughout the maze. Another element is the 2d array that is my maze. By numbering each one, I colored the sections the iconic blue or black.

5. Most Challenging Part

One thing that I had trouble with is figuring out the order of when to draw what. A lot of times, I would draw my ghost before the maze, and the maze would be painted on top, thus making it look like that there was no ghost to begin with. After a lot of time spent looking over code, and help from Wendy, I figured out how to order everything.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I had more time and patience, then I would add code that keeps the cherry from moving to a spot near my PacGirl, and make my ghosts smarter, so that they could follow PacGirl if they get within a certain distance.
